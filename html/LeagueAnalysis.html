League Analysis

Background

There are many leagues in the data set and each league is different from fans' energy to the players skill level. We believe that each league will have different level competitiveness making some leagues more attractive than others for both the audience and players. We determine the difference in competitiveness by looking at the distribution of the player's overall rating. In addition, by analyzing the distribution of players in each league, we will be able to find the quantitative results that tells us the benchmark of each league and deeper understanding of top tier players.

Findings & Conclusion

From the boxplot we found that each league has different player distribution, thus, different minimum requirements to enter the league and different competitiveness. We were able to identify similar leagues with similar player skill levels that managers can use to consider when starting to search for players to transfer. More importantly, by analyzing the interquartile range and the distribution of the players skill level, we were able to determine the exact quantitative result to differentiate a top tier player for each league. Overall we were able to analyze the difference in leagues and create a tool that can be use in early stages of player transferring process for the clubs in the soccer world.
